The objective of project one is to merge one of the coding topics with design aesthetics, to create a combination of interactive designs through coding.



I chose the fairy-core aesthetic because I enjoyed fairytales as a kid. This aesthetic spoke to a part of my brain that wanted to step into a whimsical world.



Tate.org shows that the fairy's first representation occurred in the pre-Victorian era by Henry Fuseli, 1790. The painting is titled "titania and bottom." Fairy paintings began to gain more traction in the 1840s during the Romanticism art movement.



According to the book "The Age of Romanticism" by Joanne Schneider, this movement was a reaction against rationalism. Romanticism allowed artists to delve into the world of dreams and the supernatural. The booming fairy depictions simmered down for many years after this movement.



However, it has rebranded itself in a new way from 2018 to 2020 as "Fairy-Core." (stemming from another loosely similar aesthetic, named Cottage-Core.



According to DazeDigital). This aesthetic takes the idea of whimsy and magic to a new extreme. It contains design elements revolving around stars, nature, flowers, sparkling creatures, butterflies, and magic.



I chose to portray this aesthetic using webcam filters and AR. The journal of physics defines augmented reality as a technology that combines virtual information with the real world through multimedia, real-time tracking, and sensing interactions. In my research, I found there is a parallel between the fairy core aesthetic and AR webcam filters.



The fairy-core aesthetic involves the idea of romanticism, things that invoke the supernatural and the otherworldly, and webcam filters along with AR, produce a sense of false reality, bringing to life something that is not truly there. Through a screen.



At the start, I looked at the codes presented during class and began by altering them to fit my project's theme. This process mostly involved changing the size, color, and the number of elements, and then I would remove the background to allow the camera view to be on the screen. As I progressed, I would look at different projects.



However, there were two that inspired me the most. One project is named Fairy Magic by InLifeSize, an app that uses AR to allow the user to visualize magical elements in real space. Another project is by a creative coder Charlie Gerard, and it is one that uses Pose-Net detection to interact with a screen to play the game fruit ninja.



I used tutorials from different coders online, such as TheCodingTrain and ByteSizedCoding, to understand how Pose-Net detection works and created many iterations and sketches using p5.js and my digital iPad. I also found it helpful to look at the p5js reference page to understand specific things within a code.



The design solution is an interactive filter that depicts fairies flying around the users' room. The user aims to catch the fairies using a net, which is transposed onto the hand.



There are music, flowers, and floating clouds, which create a magical and whimsical scene and transport the user into the new and playful land of fairies and webcam AR.
